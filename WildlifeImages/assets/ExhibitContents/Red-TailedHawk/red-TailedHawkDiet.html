Red-Tailed Hawk

The Red-Tailed Hawk is an opportunistic feeder. Its diet is mainly small mammals, but it also includes birds and reptiles. Prey varies with regional and seasonal availability, but usually centers on rodents, comprising up to 85% of a hawk's diet.

Most commonly reported prey types include mice, voles, chipmunks, ground squirrels, tree squirrels and woodchucks. Weighing at 1.5 to 3.5 lbs, they have a wingspan of 43 to 57 inches.
