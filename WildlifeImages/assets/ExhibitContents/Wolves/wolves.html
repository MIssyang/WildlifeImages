Gray Wolves

Gray Wolves do not have to be grey. They can be black, white, or any shade in between, but they all have a thick layer of insulating fur next to their skin, and that’s always grey. Anatomically and physiologically, wolves are built for traveling long distances.

They have long, strong legs and hard pads on the soles of their paws. Their cardiovascular and respiratory systems are made for endurance. They spend a lot of time chasing down prey and patrolling their territory. A single pack may have a home range of 800 square miles.
