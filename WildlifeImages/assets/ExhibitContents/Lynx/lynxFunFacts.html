Fun Facts

Did you know? The name “Lynx” comes from the Greek word “to shine”. The name might be in reference to its eye shine at night.

Although the largest lynx species, it is a medium-sized cat.

The stealthy lynx avoids humans and hunts at night, so they are rarely seen.

Lynx are skilled hunters, agile climbers, and Wow! Like the water and are good swimmers.

The tufts of fur on the tips of their ears are like a hearing aid and amplify the sound. Interesting!

Their eyesight is so strong that a lynx can spot a mouse 250 feet away. Amazing!

Although not fast runners, they can leap up to 10 feet in the air. Unbelievable!

Lynx are carnivores with a diet that consists of: deer, hares, foxes, rodents, birds and their eggs.

Lynx can make a variety of sounds similar to those of a housecat: Mews, yowls, hisses, and purs. Really!

Lynx live in dens they make in hollow logs, beneath roots, and other sheltered places.

Although they aren’t endangered, they remain a species of concern due to the abundance of illegal lynx fur trade. Humans remain their largest predator.
