Common Barn Owl

Barn Owls are the most common species of owl, found on every continent, except Antarctica. They are quite adaptable, & can live in a variety of habitats. Natural roosting sites include caves & tree hollows, but it’s not uncommon for them to take up residence in an abandoned building or barn.

They are referred to as Nature’s Mousetrap because of their efficiency at catching mice & rats. Weighing a little less than 1 pound themselves, they will eat up to their body weight in mice a night!
