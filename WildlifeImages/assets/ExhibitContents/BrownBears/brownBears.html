Brown Bears

There are 2 subspecies of the Brown Bears. The grizzly lives throughout the mainland of Alaska, throughout Canada, and in a couple of isolated populations in the lower 48 states. The Kodiak lives only on the Kodiak Archipelago. Grizzlies usually weigh 800-1200 pounds. Kodiaks, because of their salmon-rich diet, often exceed 1500 pounds.

The diet of brown bear is about half meat and half vegetation. Unlike the black bear, which is fairly non-aggressive, and can tolerate living in the same enclosure with other black bears, the brown bear is more territorial; not in the sense of marking and defending a specific territory, but they are very defensive of their personal space, and they need a lot of it.
